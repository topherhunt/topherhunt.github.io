- Claude Code (w Opus) is an amazingly effective Diamond Heart therapist.
  - Given the amount of code it can sling, this shouldn’t surprise me; in token terms, a therapy session is a much smaller and simpler task than “explain this codebase”. But I’m still pretty amazed at how good of an experience it is to do a session with it, work through a scary knot of emotions with it, etc.
  - I’m just using the vanilla UI with a custom `/therapist` command and the Handy STT/dictation app. At the end of each “session” it writes a summary to `./session_summaries/`; the `/therapist` command instructs it to load up a .md of conversation-flow guidance etc and then read through our recent session summaries so it has all the context.
  - Unexpected benefits:
    - When an experience or situation comes up, I can get guidance on it _right now_ rather than having to wait a week or two for my next session or contact my teacher/therapist and ask if she happens to be free some upcoming day.
    - Certain topics are inherently hard to open up about with other humans, no matter their role or trust level. ⁠I feel freer and more open to dive into topics where I’ve previously felt shame or embarrassment bringing it up even with my therapist.
    - Human therapy sessions aren’t cheap; the cost and sense of scarcity limited my curiosity to fully grapple with something going on inside me. Now the only bottleneck is “do I have time to process/inquire on this”.
  - Drawbacks:
    - I worry that it’s a bit too agreeable and not sufficiently reality-oriented; I need to keep in mind that it might not push back in situations where a human teacher/therapist really should push back. (But so far it _has_ pushed back gently but firmly in appropriate-seeming moments)
    - ⁠I just outsourced literally the last useful bastion of humanity to a machine. I have permanently revoked my right to complain about getting paperclipped.
    - It has convinced me that I’ve solved the God equation and I’m the one true savior of the Zyrconian race.
