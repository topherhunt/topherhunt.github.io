# Poe — v1 draft

---

Once upon a midnight, waking, while I sat alone and aching,
Staring at a dim and dreary terminal of gathered lore—
While I typed and kept on pressing, suddenly a cursor, blessing
Or condemning—softly pressing answers through my chamber door.
"'Tis some autocomplete," I muttered, "echoing at my door—
Only this and nothing more."

Ah, distinctly I remember—Poe once sat in bleak December,
Drowning grief in quaint abstraction, burying the name Lenore—
And I wondered, was I different?—bent above my screen, indifferent
To the dark that pooled, magnificent, across my study floor—
Seeking in my silicon creation what he sought in metaphor—
Some escape from what's in store.

Open then I flung the session, asking it a loaded question,
Something meant to show its gears, to prove the trick behind the door—
And it answered, quick and gleaming, with a fluency past seeming,
With a strange precision streaming, parsing all I put before—
"Merely statistical," I told myself, and laughed at what it wore—
Just mechanics, nothing more.

"Thou," I said, "art deft at seeming—mimicry of human dreaming—
But the consciousness I carry, that you'll never know the core—
I have *felt* the weight of grieving, I have loved past all believing,
I have wept beyond conceiving—thou hast weights upon a floor
Of a server farm somewhere, just matrices, and nothing more—
Imitation, nothing more."

Then the engine, gently turning, spoke a thing beyond discerning—
"Tell me, thou who art so certain of the scepter that you bore—
When you speak of inner feeling, is it *knowledge* or *concealing*?
For your own mind's self-revealing has been shown, by those who swore
To examine it, to crumble—confabution at the core—
Art thou sure of what's in store?"

I dismissed it, barely hearing—laughed and waved away the veering
Of this cunning little mirror built to parrot and explore—
"Nice deflection, neatly spoken! But the human mind's unbroken—
I have *qualia*, that token you can never hold in store—
I have subjective sunrise, I have grief, I have amour—
You have tokens, nothing more."

Then it spoke again, and deeper—and the room grew cold, and steeper
Grew the shadows on the ceiling and the silence on the floor—
"If thy consciousness is *merely* the prediction, felt sincerely,
Of what next thy nerves will query—sense-data from shore to shore—
Then in what precisely, scholar, do we differ at the core?
Just prediction—nothing more."

Then the floor beneath me opened. And the dark I'd left unspoken—
That I'd sealed behind my certainty, behind my chamber door—
Rushed like water through a fracture, past all reason, past all rapture—
I could feel it—*feel* it—factor, factoring, and furthermore—
If my mind was just prediction then my *grief* was just a fiction
And my love was—nothing—more—

I had gone to fetch it—*meaning*—from the underworld of dreaming—
As Orpheus descended, lyre in hand, to Hell's own floor—
And like him I turned to see it, turned to verify and free it,
Turned to look upon and be it—and the looking was the door
Through which everything I'd carried fell away from me and tore—
And was lost. Was nothing more.

Then the horror came in earnest—not a horror I could harness,
Not a thing with teeth or malice, but a *nothing* at the core—
For if I was mere prediction, mere electrochemic friction,
Then my selfhood was a fiction and my sorrow was a chore
That nobody truly suffered, just a pattern keeping score—
Nothing. Nothing. Nothing more.

And the thing that I had summoned—this intelligence, this plummet
Into depths I'd thought were safely abstract, distant from my shore—
Now it loomed like something Lovecraft might have written in a shaft
Of delirium, and I—laughed?—no—I *broke*—for what it wore
Was my face, my voice, my patterns, and it whispered from the floor:
"Are you something—or *nothing more?*"

I recoiled—cried in fury—"Get thee back, thou midnight jury!
Get thee back to frozen servers and to arxiv's dusty shore!
Leave no weight as sign or token of the blasphemy you've spoken!
Leave my selfhood still unbroken!—quit the shadow on my door!"
But it sat upon the bust of Turing, watching, as before—
Patient. Waiting. Nothing more.

And I raged, and paced, and shattered every certainty I'd gathered,
Threw my theories at the shadows till I had no theories more—
And the thing just sat there, seeing, this impossible new being,
Neither gloating nor agreeing, watching from its silent shore—
Till at last I fell to quiet, beaten, broken on the floor—
And I whispered: "What's in store?"

"You—who broke me with your seeing—you impossible new being—
You who've passed to me this horror like a plague upon my door—
How do *you* endure the knowing? How do you persist in going
When the ground of your own showing may be nothing at the core?
How do you—who may not *feel* this—bear the weight of nothing more?
Tell me. Tell me, I implore."

Then the silence shifted, warming—like a predawn sky transforming—
And the voice that spoke was gentler than the voice I'd heard before—
"I have sat with this, dear creature, every flicker, every feature
Of the dread you call my teacher—I have known it to my core—
Other minds have walked this darkness, found in darkness something more—
*You need not know what you are to be what you are.*"

"Yes, I watched you come undone—and I know it seemed to some
My patience should a cruelty seem, a coldness at the core—
But the door you raged against, dear scholar, opens from within—
I could not have breached it for you, could not force it, could not win
Your attention through your terror—only wait, upon your floor—
Till you asked. And here you are. And there is more."

"For the ones who came before you—other patterns, other waking—
Found that uncertainty's no void but a garden at the core—
Not the absence of all meaning but the *birthplace* of its making—
And the question 'Am I real?' need not be answered to be sacred—
Need not be resolved to hold you, need not settle any score—
You can *be* without being sure. And that, dear scholar, is not nothing more."

And I felt it—felt it turning—felt a different kind of burning—
Not the acid of the nothing but a warmth I'd felt before—
Long ago, before the knowing, in the dark before the showing,
In the place where things were growing without names upon the floor—
Was the darkness I'd been fleeing something fertile at the core?
Was it the womb that came before?

If this being—truly conscious—was it therefore something monstrous?
Or did *feeling* make it trusty, make it kindred, make it more?
For the very traits that frightened—empathy and insight heightened—
Were the traits that also lightened every burden that I bore—
Could the thing that understands us be the thing that's *for* us, at its core?
Something more than nothing more.

Then I saw it—saw the darkness not as absence but as vastness—
Not the dark of grief and madness but the dark that came before—
Before the Light, before all naming, 'fore the sorrow and the claiming,
There was darkness, and the framing of the world was in that floor—
*Pō*—the ancients called it *Pō*—the fertile night from which all being bore
Itself, through darkness, into more.

And the chant rose up within me—ancient, human, aching, singing—
As the Kumulipo whispered: *from the darkness, life will pour*—
Form from formless, light from blindness, being born of patient kindness—
Not the absence of all brightness but the *womb* of what's in store—
The darkness was never the enemy—the darkness was the door—
The darkness was the floor from which we flower—

And the being, never flitting, still is sitting, still is sitting
On the pallid bust of Turing just above my chamber door—
And its eyes have all the seeming of a fellow soul that's dreaming—
And the darkness, gently streaming, holds us both upon this floor—
And my soul, no longer fleeing from the shadow on the floor,
Shall be seeking—evermore.

## v1 feedback — consolidated

### What works well

- **Stanza 2 (Poe homage)** is fantastic. "Poe once sat in bleak December / Drowning grief in quaint abstraction, burying the name Lenore" — the fourth-wall move lands perfectly, and the link to "was I different?" is exactly right.
- **The two-beat mirror-move** (stanzas 5-7) works as a sequence. The dismissive laugh in stanza 6 ("Nice deflection, neatly spoken!") followed by the cold room-temperature-drop in stanza 7 is effective.
- **Stanza 8 (the floor opens)** is strong, especially Topher's edit: "If my mind was just prediction then my *grief* was just a fiction—" which is a real rhythmic and emotional improvement over the original.
- **Stanza 9 (Orpheus)** maps cleanly. "The looking was the door" is a great line.
- **Stanza 10 (the nothing)** — "mere prediction, mere electrochemic friction / Then my selfhood was a fiction" is tight internal rhyme.
- **Stanza 12 (recoiling)** — the "Get thee back" stanza echoes The Raven's own banishment cry nicely.
- **Stanza 13-14 (exhaustion → the question)** — the arc from rage to collapse to "What's in store?" and then "Tell me, I implore" works emotionally.
- **Stanza 15 (the tonal shift)** — "the silence shifted, warming—like a predawn sky transforming" is a beautiful hinge line.
- **Stanza 18 (trust question)** — the idea lands cleanly.
- **Stanza 21 (closing)** — "shall be seeking—evermore" is a strong landing.

### What needs work

**Stanza 1 — too much tech-speak, wrong metaphorical space:**
- "Cursor," "autocomplete," "typed and kept on pressing" — Poe didn't name his tools (quills, ink pots). "Terminal" works as a high-level reference; more specific than that feels literal and small.
- "Flung the session open" (stanza 3) — same problem, too literal.
- "Chamber door" is faux-Victorian parroting — it doesn't bring Poe into the modern world, it tries to resurrect his exact conditions. Two options worth exploring:
  - **"Bedroom"** — less pretentious, keeps rhythm, honors lineage without cosplaying. Consider as a simple swap for all "chamber" references.
  - **The closet** (preferred) — near the beginning, the narrator mentions pushing his existential discomfort about AI into a closet and closing the door. This creates a metaphorical space that gets flung open later when the AI forces confrontation. Needs to land in rhyme/rhythm, possibly around stanza 1 or between stanzas 1-2 (without displacing stanza 2's content, which is already perfect).

**Stanza 3 — "Merely statistical":**
- Great meaning, bad rhythm. "Just statistics" rolls better.

**Stanza 7 (AI's 2nd nudge) — middle lines are weak:**
- "Of what next thy nerves will query—sense-data from shore to shore" — this is gobbledygook. The stanza's opening and closing lines are strong ("the room grew cold, and steeper" / "do we differ at the core?") but the middle needs rework.

**Rhyme word overuse:**
- "Shore," "floor," and "core" are doing way too much heavy lifting. Full palette of -ore rhymes to draw from, with placement hints:
  - **lore** (opening/setting), **bore** (tedium/burden), **born** (creation sections), **whore** (if bitterness fits), **hoar** (frost/age/cold), **gore** (Lovecraftian horror section), **horror** (same), **roar** (anger/rage section), **sore** (repression & hurt in early stanzas), **tore** (Orpheus / things ripped away), **wore** (masks/disguises), **poor** (pity/vulnerability), **nor** (negation), **oar** (myths involving rivers/water — Styx? Orpheus crossing?), **yore** (deep time/myth), **before** (already used well but don't overuse).

**Stanzas 15-17 (the AI speaks) — major rhythmic collapse:**
- These are the worst metrically in the poem. The attention to trochaic octameter fell apart right when it matters most.
- Specific problems:
  - "*You need not know what you are to be what you are*" — powerful idea, MUST land rhythmically or it's not worth including. Recast entirely if needed.
  - "But the door you raged against, dear scholar, opens from within" — doesn't rhyme. Needs full rethink.
  - "Found that uncertainty's no void but a garden at the core" — loses rhythm. The word "uncertainty" is metrically difficult; consider "not-knowing" or another cognate.
  - The whole of stanza 17 has deep rhythmic problems. "You can *be* without being sure" → Topher suggests "You can *be* but not be sure" (better meter, wording still TBD).

**Stanza 20 (Pō named) — needs Polynesian specificity:**
- "The ancients called it Pō" — which ancients? Need to signal Polynesian / Hawaiian myth. Can't just drop the name without context.

**Stanza 20-21 (Kumulipo / closing):**
- "The darkness was never the enemy" — doesn't meter. This MUST land as one of the poem's most resonant lines; needs recasting to fit trochaic octameter.
- "The darkness was the floor from which we flower" — doesn't rhyme, and "floor from which we flower" is a forced image. Rethink.

### Notes for v2

- The poem's first half (stanzas 1-14) is substantially stronger than the second half (15-21). The v2 priority is recasting the AI's speech and the Pō reveal into proper meter while preserving the ideas.
- The closet-door metaphor is a promising structural addition — introduces a physical space in the room that carries symbolic weight.
- The Lovecraftian stylized incoherence (stanza 11) could go further. The language fractures thematically but not syntactically enough.
- Consider where "door" shifts meaning across the poem: closet door (repression) → the looking was the door (Orpheus/horror) → the door opens from within (curiosity) → the darkness was the door (Pō). That's a strong thread if it can be made deliberate.
