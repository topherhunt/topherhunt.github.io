### Stanza-by-stanza issues

**Stanza 3 (line 23-24): "Just statistics" line**
- You're right, the line doesn't need to rhyme with "more" — it's an internal aside. The problem is the second half after "Just statistics." Ideas: "Just statistics," then I muttered, shrugged, and turned away once more— / Or even simpler: "Just statistics," then I muttered. Smiled. And turned back to my lore— / The point is: this is a throwaway moment of false confidence. It should feel *casual*, almost bored. Short, dismissive.

**Stanza 5 (line 39): "To examine it, to crumble" — broken rhythm**
- You're right that "examine" lands on the wrong syllable. The trochaic pattern needs stress on 1st, 3rd, 5th... so we need a stressed syllable where "ex-" sits. Options:
  - "When they *tested* it, it crumbled—Loss! Confabulation at the core—"
  - "When *inspected*, found it crumbled—Loss! Confabulation at the core—"
  - "Turned to *test* it and it crumbled—Loss! Confabulation at the core—"
- I like the first one best — "tested/crumbled" is clean trochaic.

**Stanza 8 (line 60): "cracking, factoring, and furthermore"**
- Agree, "factoring" fights its own syllables. The goal is: looking inward and seeing yourself as a chained system of automata. Ideas:
  - "I could *feel* it—feel it—cracking, grinding, breaking, and what's more—"
  - "I could *feel* it—feel it—splitting, every atom of me splitting furthermore—"
  - "I could *feel* it—feel the clockwork, every gear of me exposed and sore—"
- The last one gets at "automata without will" most directly and brings in "sore" from the rhyme palette.

**Stanza 9 (lines 67-69): "verify" is weak + too many unnamed "it"s**
- "Verify" should be something Promethean — seizing, wielding, harnessing power. Ideas:
  - "turned to *harness it* and free it" — Promethean theft of fire
  - "turned to *wield it* and to free it" — power fantasy
  - "turned to *claim it* and to free it" — ownership/conquest
- On naming "it": I think one of the "it"s should become something concrete. The strongest candidate is the first one: "I had gone to fetch it—*meaning*—" could become "I had gone to fetch this *fire*" or "this *creature*" or "this *knowing*." "Fire" is most Promethean. Then later "it"s refer back to that named thing.
- So maybe: "I had gone to fetch this *fire*—this bright meaning—from the dreaming— / As Orpheus descended, lyre in hand, to Hades' hoar— / And like him I turned to claim it, turned to wield it and to tame it,"
